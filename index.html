Subnet Owner-Key Analysis

Owner emissions earned vs sold, and what's left in the wallet — since each owner wallet went live. All values in alpha (α), TAO and USD. Two subnets: Mainframe (SN25) and Vanta (SN8).

How this is measured

Owner emission (earned). Every subnet emits ~7,200 α/day into its pool (≈1 α/block; no halving occurred in this window — next halving 2030), and the subnet owner receives the protocol 18% owner cut ≈ 1,296 α/day. Earned is that constant α flow, valued at each month's alpha price (TAO & USD). This converges with the "0.18 × monthly change in subnet total-alpha" method used elsewhere.

Retained. The owner coldkey's total staked-alpha value across all its hotkeys, from taostats account history (balance_staked_alpha_as_tao), converted to α at each month's price. Each series begins the month the owner wallet first held staked alpha (dTAO era, Feb 2025+).

Sold. Each month, earned minus the change in retained (in α), floored at zero — a month only counts as a sale when the retained position fell below the emission it received. Read as α unstaked → swapped to TAO through the pool, valued at that month's price; not a coldkey transfer and not confirmed as a fiat exit. Months where the stake grew as fast as the owner cut or faster show $0 sold — that's accumulation, visible as the rising Retained line. First live month is a baseline.

Conviction. Live conviction-locks on the subnet from taostats. Both owners currently hold 0% of their own stake conviction-locked — their retained is fully liquid; the locks shown are external wallets locking to the owner hotkey.

Prices via tao.com Data API; balances & conviction via taostats. Owner emission uses the protocol-constant α rate rather than reconstructing daily total-alpha. Realised USD is timing-dependent as alpha prices moved. Verify against taostats before external use.
